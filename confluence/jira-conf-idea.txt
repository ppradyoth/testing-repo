

### 🚨 Problem

Today, outputs from **AI Threat Modelling** and **AI Red Teaming exercises** are primarily captured in Jira comments and discussions.

This leads to:

* Fragmented visibility across findings
* Manual effort to consolidate insights
* Limited ability to track remediation timelines or trends
* No centralized, queryable repository of AI/ML security risks

As our AI security efforts scale, this becomes a bottleneck for both **governance and execution**.

---

### 💡 Proposed Solution

Build an **automated pipeline between Jira and Confluence** that transforms unstructured findings into a centralized, structured knowledge base.

**Workflow:**

* When a Jira task (Threat Modelling / Red Teaming) transitions to *Done*
* The system extracts structured findings from the latest comment
* Parses and normalizes them into a standard schema
* Automatically updates a **Confluence Findings Tracker (acting as a live database)**

---

### 🧠 Key Capabilities

* 📌 Centralized tracking of all AI/ML security findings
* 🔍 Structured data model (Finding ID, Severity, Description, Source, etc.)
* ⏱️ Automated timestamps and duration tracking (time-to-detect, time-to-close)
* 🔄 Real-time updates with zero manual intervention
* 📊 Foundation for dashboards and reporting

---

### 📈 Business Impact

* **Improved Visibility:** Leadership gets a unified view of AI risk posture
* **Operational Efficiency:** Eliminates manual tracking and duplication
* **Faster Remediation:** Clear ownership and lifecycle tracking
* **Scalability:** Supports growing volume of AI security assessments
* **Data-Driven Decisions:** Enables trend analysis (e.g., recurring vulnerabilities, high-risk areas)

---

### 🔐 Strategic Value

This positions the CDAO PSL team to:

* Operationalize **AI security as a measurable discipline**
* Build a **living knowledge base of adversarial patterns**
* Lay the groundwork for advanced capabilities like:

  * Automated risk scoring (CVSS for AI)
  * OWASP Top 10 for LLMs mapping
  * Predictive risk analytics

---

### ⚙️ Implementation Approach

* Jira Automation → Webhook trigger on task completion
* Lightweight service (Python/FastAPI) for parsing and transformation
* Integration with Confluence APIs for structured updates
* Optional backend datastore for scalability and analytics
