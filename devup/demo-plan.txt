Normal Ops

Failure Injected
The payment gateway experiences a timeout due to a downstream vendor issue.
Alerts fire (visible in the observability dashboard).
AI Layer Kicks In
A small AI model (pre-trained on historical failures) analyzes the telemetry.
It detects: "Timeout pattern matches vendor X outage, not internal code issue."
AI generates a recommendation: "Retry with exponential backoff, up to 3 attempts."
This recommendation is sent to the blockchain layer.
Blockchain Layer Enforces Policy
A smart contract (pre-approved by JPMorgan's risk committee) evaluates the event.
It checks the policy: "For payment timeouts, if vendor X, retry up to 3 times, then escalate."
The contract approves the retry action and records the decision on the immutable ledger.
On screen: a new block appears with the hash of the incident, the AI's recommendation, and the policy rule.
Automated Execution
The execution layer (integrated with Kubernetes/GitOps) triggers a retry.
The system attempts the payment again.
Success! The transaction goes through.
The outcome is recorded back to the ledger.
